KOLLAM

Kollam known by its former name Quilon, is an ancient seaport and city on the  Malabar Coast of India bordering the Laccadive Sea, which is a part of the  Arabian Sea .It is situated 66 kilometers north of the state capital Trivandrum. The city is on the banks of Ashtamudi Lake and the Kallada river.
British Residency

The British Residency also known as the Government Guest House or Residency Bungalow, is a two-storeyed palace situated at Asramam in the city of Kollam in the Indian state of Kerala. It lies close to the site of the old Kollam Airport situated at Asramam in the city and was built by Colonel John Munro between 1811 and 1819. It is a noted Kollam landmark like the Chinnakada Clock Tower.

Ashtamudi Lake

Ashtamudi Lake is the most visited backwater and lake in the state. It possesses a unique wetland ecosystem and a large palm-shaped (also described as octopus-shaped) water body, second only in size to the Vembanad estuary ecosystem of the state.

Kollam Port

Kollam Port is one of the historic ports. It is the second largest port in Kerala by volume of cargo handled and facilities. It is the second largest port in Kerala by volume of cargo handled and facilities.
