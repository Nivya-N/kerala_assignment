ERNAKULAM

Ernakulam, is known as the commercial capital of Kerala. Sprawling, residential Ernakulam is known for Marine Drive, a busy waterfront promenade where boats offer backwater cruises. Ernakulam encompasses an array of magnificent attractions.
Hill Palace

Hillpalace was the residence of the Maharaja of Cochin, built in traditional Kerala style with lavish façade. The complex houses 49 buildings spread across 54 acres of land which now comprises of an archaeological museum, deer park, Medicinal plant garden, pre-historic park and a children’s park.

Folklore Museum

The museum is dedicated to Folk art, Tribal art, Traditional art, Anthropology, Architecture, History, Culture, and Heritage of South India. The museum is promoted by Mrs. & Mr George Thaliath and aims to preserve, the culture, heritage and folk art education of Kerala.

Jew Synagogue and Jew Town

The Jewish Synagogue also known as the Paradesi Synagogue is the oldest active Synagogue in the Commonwealth of Nations making it one of the hotspots amongst Kochi tourist places. The Synagogue has Scrolls of the law, several gold crowns received as gifts, a brass-railed pulpit, Belgian glass chandelier, hand-knotted Oriental rug
