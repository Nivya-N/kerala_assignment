ALAPPUZHA

Alappuzha is the smallest district in Kerala. The district is best known for its picturesque backwaters, by which it is well connected to other parts of Kerala. The district is also known for its coir factories, as most of Kerala's coir industries are situated in and around Alappuzha town.
Marari Beach

One of the great delights of the Malabar Coast, the Marari Beach is an idyllic serene beach. About 11 km from the city of Alleppey, this beach is a hotspot for fishing activity, deriving its name from the local fishing village Mararikulam.

Krishnapuram Palace

Built during the reign of Travancore king Marthanda Varma, Krishnapuram Palace is a two-storey structure crafted in traditional Kerala architectural style.

Vembanad Lake

Vembanad (Vembanad Kayal or Vembanad Kol) is the longest lake in India. The Nehru Trophy Boat Race is conducted in a portion of the lake. High levels of pollution have been noticed at certain hotspots of the Vembanad backwaters. The Government of India has identified the Vembanad wetland under the National Wetlands Conservation Programme.
